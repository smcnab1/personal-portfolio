I am a dedicated healthcare professional with a strong background as a paramedic and educator, currently working as a lecturer specialising in simulation and immersive technologies. My career began in the UK Armed Forces as a reservist, which provided me with a solid foundation in both leadership and medical practice. Over time, I transitioned into the education sector, where I have developed a keen interest in using simulation and technology to enhance healthcare training.

In my current role, I focus on creating innovative and immersive learning environments that equip students and healthcare professionals with the skills needed for effective practice. My work at the University of West London, where I lead on simulation technologies, and my ongoing projects, including the development of e-learning induction packages for the simulation centre, reflect my commitment to advancing education through technology. I also contribute to wider initiatives, such as supporting neurodivergent staff and promoting recruitment efforts within the Armed Forces community.

I am submitting this portfolio for CMALT accreditation to recognise my expertise in learning technologies and my desire to further solidify my role in shaping the future of healthcare education. Achieving CMALT status aligns with my aspirations to lead the integration of advanced technologies in educational frameworks and to continue contributing to the development of both staff and students in healthcare.
